    It is with deep sadness that we inform you of the recent passing of Randy Lemmon on January 5, 2023. The former Houston radio host was beloved by many and made a huge impact on the country music world. For over 25 years, Randy Lemmon has served as the Garden Guru for Houston and the Gulf Coast. His sound advice on lawns, gardens, and outdoor living in this unique climate has provided a perfect balance between science-based information and practical experience.

    Keep reading to know more about Randy Lemmon, including who he was, how he died, and tributes to Randy Lemmon.

    Who was Randy Lemmon?

    Randy Lemmon had a deep love for his family, starting with his roots in Whittier, California. Born on January 4th 1962, he was the son of Richard and Phyllis Lemmon. Later his family relocated to Houston, where Randy finished high school at Robert E. Lee and then graduated from Texas A&M University with a Bachelor’s in Journalism in 1984. Fittingly while studying at A&M he lived at the Moses Hall residence. Adding to the impressive list of accomplishments during his career was also a Master’s degree in Agriculture obtained while working for their renowned College of Agriculture. But at the end of the day, what mattered most to him was spending time with his children, Selena and Randal; supporting them both as Selena pursued her dream of becoming a musician and Randal devoted himself to tennis competitions all over the world.

    How did Randy Lemmon die? 

    The Houston community was shocked on January 5th when Randy Lemmon, a beloved radio host and personality, passed away. An official post from his Facebook page, GardenLine with Randy Lemmon, confirmed his death at Mt. Sinai Hospital in New York City after complications due to a stroke. It is clear that he was surrounded by family throughout the difficult time and that their love for him was strong and unwavering until the very end.

    A giant of Houston’s airwaves for many years prior to his death, Randy Lemmon will be deeply missed by all those who listened to him during his long career spent delighting them with gardening tips and advice. He leaves behind a legacy of dedicated service to the community as well as an unforgettable memory in the hearts of all those who loved him most.

    When it comes to gardening and outdoor living in Houston, Texas, Randy Lemmon was one of the most trusted authorities. He led a successful radio show on GardenLine for 27 years, during which he gave helpful advice and tips about dealing with the local climate.

    Randy was commended by both professional landscapers and the amateurs who wanted to perfect their backyard projects. He even stepped forward as a go-to source when the public needed protection from natural disasters like hurricanes and severe cold waves. His passion for sharing his knowledge set him apart from all others.

    Randy Lemmon Cause of Death

    Randy Lemmon was an iconic radio host, widely known for being the Garden Guru in Houston and the Gulf Coast. He was a beloved husband, father, and uncle, having been married to Yvonne Lemmon for 22 years and leaving behind two loving children, Selena and Randal. He would often be seen attending their sports games or activities whenever possible. Unfortunately, Randy passed away due to a stroke.

    In addition to his family, he leaves behind his sister Terri Langer (Ron) as well as sisters-in-law Jennifer Spriggs (Kevin) and Melissa Salce (Joab). His nieces and nephews – Lindsey Langer, Lauren Langer., Jessica Spriggs, Jamie Spriggs, Paighton Salce, Preston Salce and Parker Salce – will deeply miss him. His love of gardening enabled others to connect with nature in a way they probably never had before.

    His aunt and uncle, Janelle and Ron Stokes, certainly feel that loss, along with their two sons, Conner and Reid. For over 35 years, Randy showed those lucky enough to benefit from his counsel the way forward when it comes to lawns, gardens, and outdoor life in this particular climate. He combined research with a deft understanding of how these elements worked together in perfect harmony, leaving an imprint on this area that will remain a solid reminder of his passing.
