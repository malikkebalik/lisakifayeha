    Who is Dean McDermott?

    Dean McDermott is a Canadian-American actor.

    People mostly know him as a reality television personality and as the host of the cooking competition ‘Chopped Canada’.

    Furthermore, he played the role of Constable Renfield Turnbull in the TV series ‘Due South’.

    How many siblings does Dean McDermott have?

    Dean was born in Toronto, Canada on November 16, 1966, as the son of  David and Doreen McDermott.

    Throughout his childhood years, he grew up with his three sisters, Dale, Dawn, and Dana.

    The world of show business amazed Dean since his early life and hence he eventually chose a career in show business.

    He is of Canadian-American nationality. Additionally, he has not revealed information relating to his ethnic background as of now.

    He became an American citizen at a ceremony in Los Angeles on August 21, 2010.

    Talking about his education, there are no records of the educational background of the Dean at present.

    Dean McDermott’s Career, Salary, and Net Worth

    In 1998, Dean appeared as a Best Boy in ‘Graveyard Shift II’. Since then, he has appeared in numerous movies and television series.

    Some of the movies and television series that he has been part of include ‘Nancy Drew’, ‘The Outer Limits’, ‘Jack Reed: Death and Vengeance’, ‘Due South’, ‘Power Play’, ‘What Makes a Family’, ‘Tracker’, ‘Earth: Final Conflict’, ‘The Tournament’, ‘Kiss the Bride’, ‘Slasher’, ‘CSI: Crime Scene Investigation’ and others. All in all, he has more than 80 credits as an actor.

    Apart from his acting career, Dean is also a professionally trained chef. He also released a cookbook titled ‘The Gourmet Dad’.

    In addition, he began hosting ‘Chopped Canada’ on Food Network Canada in January 2014.

    For his career in acting, Dean has garnered two nominations to his name.

    he garnered the Gemini Awards nomination in the category of Best Ensemble Performance in a Comedy Program or Series for ‘The Tournament’ in 2005.
