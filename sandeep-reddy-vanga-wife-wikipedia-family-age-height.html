    Sandeep Reddy Vanga Wikipedia

    Sandeep Reddy Vanga Wikipedia:- Sandeep Reddy Vanga was born on 25 December 1981 in Warangal, Telangana, India. Sandeep Reddy Vanga is an Indian director and screenwriter who is known for predominantly making Telugu and a couple of Hindi films. Vanga made his Telugu debut with the 2017 huge Telugu smash Arjun Reddy, starring Vijay Deverakonda, and soared to further success with its remake, the Shahid Kapoor-starrer 2019 Hindi feature Kabir Singh, which ranks among the top-grossing Hindi films of all time. Vanga left Vizag as an apprentice to study at the International Film School in Sydney (now known as AFTT - Academy of Film, Theatre, and Television) in Australia. During his studies in Sydney, he also worked part-time at Subway, where he liked spending time with colleagues Rahul, Harsha, Pratik, and Nash.

    Sandeep Reddy Vanga Career

    Sandeep Reddy Vanga Career:- Vanga spent 25 days in 2005 as an apprentice for the film Manasu Maata Vinadhu at Rushikonda Beach in Vizag. He was an assistant director on Kedi (2010) with Kiran Kumar and an associate director on Malli Malli Idi Rani Roju (2015) with Kranthi Madhav. He began writing the script for his directorial debut, Sugar Factory, but eventually shelved it and began writing the script for Arjun Reddy instead. Vanga began working on the script for Arjun Reddy in 2013 and completed it in two years. Producer Swapna Dutt and Sharwanand expressed interest in producing and acting in the film, but it did not go as planned because Sharwanand had multiple projects in the works at the time. A mutual friend introduced him to Vijay Deverakonda. In his directorial debut, Arjun Reddy, he cast him as the lead. The film was released to critical praise on August 25, 2017 and quickly became a blockbuster smash. It was made on a shoestring budget of 4-5 crores yet grossed more than 50 crores (US$8 million) at the box office.
